## Featured Projects

### B9 Robot — New Zealand's Only AI-Powered B9 Environmental Control Robot
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/b9-robot-mobile-laboratory.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/b9-robot-mobile-laboratory.html)

A fully working, AI-powered replica of the B9 Environmental Control Robot from the classic TV series *Lost in Space*, built by Michael Fenton in New Zealand. This unique interactive robot is used as a mobile science laboratory and STEM engagement tool for learners of all ages. It features embedded AI, voice control, real-time sensor data broadcasting, and support for the RIGEL application. The robot can speak te reo Māori and other languages, and is designed for classroom resilience and learner engagement. This is believed to be the only B9 robot in New Zealand.

### Casio Calculator Serial Protocol Hack — Ultra Low Cost Data Logger
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/casio-calculator-data-logger-hack.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/casio-calculator-data-logger-hack.html)

Michael Fenton pioneered timed-interval data logging using Casio FX-9750, FX-9860, and FX-CG50 calculators connected to low-cost Picaxe sensor units. He invented a method to pause RECEIVE() without a COM error, enabling timed sensor recording for under $8. This project-based learning resource scaffolds science, mathematics, and data literacy in New Zealand schools.

### Digital Multimeters as Science Data Loggers
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/digital-multimeters-as-data-loggers.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/digital-multimeters-as-data-loggers.html)

Michael pioneered the use of digital multimeters (DMM) as inexpensive science data loggers in New Zealand primary and secondary classrooms. Home-made sensors costing just a few cents can detect a wide range of environmental factors, enabling authentic hands-on inquiry learning in science and maths.

### Artificial Intelligence in Education
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/artificial-intelligence-in-education.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/artificial-intelligence-in-education.html)

Analysis of current AI use in education, including a response to Auckland University research on AI's impact on student learning outcomes and assessment design.

## About Michael Fenton

Michael Fenton is a professional member of the Royal Society of New Zealand (MRSNZ). He founded New Zealand's only secondary school-based research group, the **Nexus Research Group**, whose patron was Dr William 'Bill' Pickering, former head of NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL). The group encouraged students of all ages to conduct authentic citizen science research, which was presented at national conferences and provided to the Ministry of Education.

Michael received a Ministry of Education **e-Learning Fellowship** for his work on low-cost digital technology tools for science and mathematics education. He has been a keynote speaker at science and education conferences including SciCon, and his work has been featured in newspapers, radio, television, and education journals.

He co-designed (with Dr Christine Fenton) New Zealand's first alternative to NCEA — a New Zealand Certificate in Study and Employment Pathways (Level 3 and Level 4) approved by NZQA for Green School New Zealand, which also meets University Entrance requirements.

Michael builds interactive props from science fiction, including a TARDIS, a Dalek, a Ghostbusters proton pack, and the AI-powered B9 Environmental Control Robot.
