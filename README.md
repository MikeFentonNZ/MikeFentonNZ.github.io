## Featured Projects

### B9 Robot — New Zealand's Only AI-Powered B9 Environmental Control Robot
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/b9-robot-mobile-laboratory.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/b9-robot-mobile-laboratory.html)

A fully working, AI-powered child-friendly likeness of the B9 Environmental Control Robot from the classic TV series *Lost in Space*, built by Michael Fenton in New Zealand. This unique interactive robot is used as a mobile science laboratory and STEM engagement tool for learners of all ages. It features embedded AI, voice control, real-time sensor data broadcasting, and support for the RIGEL application. The robot can speak te reo Māori and other languages, and is designed for classroom resilience and letting children see themselves as scientists. This is believed to be the only standalone AI-enabled B9 robot in New Zealand. Michael has composed 'Roll with me B9'  music videos suitable for young and older learners, showcasing the art, creativity and fun of authentic hands-on science and maths. 

### Casio Calculator Ultra Low Cost data logger - using Picaxe, Microbit, or ESP32 
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/casio-calculator-data-logger-hack.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/casio-calculator-data-logger-hack.html)

Michael Fenton pioneered timed-interval data logging using Casio FX-9750, FX-9860, and FX-CG50 calculators connected to low-cost Picaxe sensor units. He discovered READY-gated timing windows in the Casio serial communication protocol to pause RECEIVE() without a COM error. Ultra low cost timed sensor recording using common school graphing calculators. Project-based learning resources scaffold science, mathematics, and data literacy in schools world-wide.

### Digital Multimeters as Science Data Loggers
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/digital-multimeters-as-data-loggers.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/digital-multimeters-as-data-loggers.html)

Michael pioneered the use of digital multimeters (DMM) as inexpensive science data logger substitutes in New Zealand primary and secondary classrooms. Home-made sensors costing just a few cents can detect a wide range of environmental factors. Enabling school and home-based authentic hands-on inquiry learning in science and maths.

### RIGEL — Real-World Interactive Games and Electronics Link
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/rigel-games-electronics-link.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/rigel-games-electronics-link.html)

Michael Fenton invented RIGEL, a universal real-world interface that connects low-cost DIY sensors and microcontrollers to game design software, turning a game engine into a fully functional science lab, data logger, and industrial measurement and control system. RIGEL auto-connects and recognises sensors on Picaxe, Arduino, ESP8266, and ESP32 boards. Sensors can measure temperature, pressure, UV light, infrared, magnetic fields, salinity, alpha radiation, and more — many costing just a few cents. RIGEL can speak te reo Māori and other languages, control robots remotely, simulate CPR training, and allow students to fly a 3D flight simulator using an exercise bike as the controller. Developed for New Zealand classrooms as a low-cost, scalable alternative to expensive commercial data logging software.

### Game Design and Coding for Cross-Curricula STEM Learning
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/game-design-coding-learning.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/game-design-coding-learning.html)

Michael Fenton uses accessible game design software as a vehicle for integrated STEM, arts, and music learning in New Zealand schools. His "Build it, Test it, Use it" pedagogy uses block-based and GML coding to create games that incorporate real-world sensor data, voice acting, original soundtracks, and graphic design. Classroom examples include DNA electrophoresis simulators, 3D TARDIS games, CPR timing simulators, and a 2025 remake of the classic 1982 Apple II game Transylvania — complete with an original AI-assisted soundtrack. Game design naturally integrates mathematics, physics, coding, performing arts, and music into single learner-centred projects, supporting low-floor, high-ceiling differentiated learning for all ability levels.

### Maker Science — Learning Science Through Making, Robotics, and Electronics
[https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/maker-science-robotics-electronics.html](https://mikefentonnz.github.io/projects/maker-science-robotics-electronics.html)

Michael Fenton's Maker Science pedagogy transforms abstract STEM concepts into tangible, working devices that students build, test, and use to investigate the world. Rooted in constructionist learning and inquiry-based practice, the approach uses low-cost Picaxe microcontrollers, breadboards, and DIY sensors to make sophisticated projects accessible in any New Zealand school regardless of budget. Students build social robots, environmental monitors, CPR training simulators, and game controllers from recycled parts. The Input-Process-Output framework connects electronics, biology, physics, and computer science naturally. Michael's Applied Robotics unit provides a fully documented ten-session implementation, scaling from primary to secondary level. His open-source resources and YouTube documentation share these methods with educators globally.

## About Michael Fenton

Michael Fenton (MRSNZ) is one of New Zealand's most broadly qualified and experienced STEM educators, with a career spanning all three science disciplines — biology, chemistry, and physics — as well as mathematics including calculus, taught from primary to tertiary and polytechnic level. He is also an expert in digital technology, robotics, electronics, and game design as educational tools, and an experienced STEM course designer, education researcher, and assessment expert. With approximately decades of combined research, industry, and teaching experience, he is among a very small number of New Zealand educators who combine deep subject knowledge across all sciences and mathematics with hands-on expertise in modern digital technology pedagogy.

Michael Fenton (MRSNZ) is a multi-award-winning New Zealand scientist, STEM education lecturer, and innovative teacher. He is also a science communicator and course design / assessment design consultant based in Taranaki, New Zealand. He is a professional member of the Royal Society of New Zealand, demonstrating competence to undertake independent research practice.

He founded New Zealand's only secondary school-based research group, the Nexus Research Group, whose patron was Dr William 'Bill' Pickering, former head of NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL). The group encouraged students of all ages to conduct authentic citizen science research, presented at national conferences and provided to the Ministry of Education.

Michael received a Ministry of Education e-Learning Fellowship for his pioneering work on low-cost digital technology tools for science and mathematics education. He has designed and delivered NZQA-approved Level 7 in-service teacher professional development qualifications in science and mathematics teaching, mentoring teachers and principals across New Zealand.

He has been a keynote speaker at conferences including SciCon, and his work has been featured in newspapers, radio, television, and education journals.
He co-designed (with Dr Christine Fenton) New Zealand's first approved alternative to NCEA — a New Zealand Certificate in Study and Employment Pathways (Level 3 and Level 4) for Green School New Zealand, accredited by NZQA and meeting University Entrance requirements. His philosophy of teaching to interests and abilities rather than age group has influenced educators and curriculum designers across the compulsory and vocational education sectors.

Most recently, Michael composed Roll With Me, B9 — an original song for children featuring the B9 robot as a science hero. The lyrics distil his entire teaching philosophy: that science is curiosity, play, invention, and wonder rather than facts to memorise, and that creativity and scientific thinking are not opposites but partners. It is perhaps the most concise statement of a teaching career built on that conviction.

Michael builds interactive science fiction props including a TARDIS, a Dalek, a Ghostbusters proton pack, and New Zealand's only AI-powered B9 Environmental Control Robot — all used as authentic STEM engagement tools.
